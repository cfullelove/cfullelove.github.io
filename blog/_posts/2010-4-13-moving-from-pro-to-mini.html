--- layout: post title: Moving from Pro to Mini post_date: 2010-04-13 07:20:00 ---

For almost 2 years now I have used a MacBook Pro as my primary computer. I am a very big fan of Apple products, especially the Mac OS X operating system. Sadly, my priorities have changed and I am "downgrading" to a Mac Mini.



When I bought my Macbook Pro, I intended to use it as a desktop replacement. That is, I used it with a external monitor, keyboard and mouse attached on a desk and rarely moved it. I bought the Macbook Pro because I still wanted the option to take it to Uni when the need arose. But this situation has now changed.

I would be lying if I said the release of the iPad didn't have an effect on my decision to sell my Macbook. My rationale is this: I kept using my Macbook to keep the mobility option available. With the imminent  release of the iPad (in Australia), I see an opportunity to improve my desktop experience (the Mac Mini) and still have the option of mobility (the iPad) without spending an excessive amount of money.


Here is my more detailed thinking:
I bought my Macbook Pro for about $2000. I hope to sell it for a little over $1000. So effectively I'm loosing ~$900. Considering I got about 2 years of good used out of this, that is a reasonable loss in my opinion.
The Mac Mini (that I recently purchased) cost $1050. The iPad, that I'm hoping to purchase, I expect to cost around $600.
I suppose my thinking is that I'm replacing my laptop with two devices that combined cost less than purchased my laptop for and will hopefully give me a better range of use.

So far the Mac Mini has met my expectations and needs perfectly. Its TINY and has more than enough grunt to do anything that I ask it. Though, admittedly, I'm not a gamer...

I am very much looking forward to getting my hands on the iPad. From what I heard a lot of people REALLY like it. And I get the impression I will to. Whether or not that is because I'm a sucker for the hype remains to be seen. Some people, on the other hand, were not as impressed. On his blog, Jeff Jarvis posted a reboxing video and gave a quick word on why.
