--- layout: post title: Lab Access! post_date: 2010-04-23 08:47:05 ---

As you are probably aware, I am in my pseudo-final year of my Bachelor of Engineering degree. In that final year students work on a year long project relating to their chosen field on study. I am currently working on mine. Unfortunately, however, my efforts to achieve any significant amount of progress has been restricted by, among other smaller issues, insufficient access to thesis labs.

As a student going a thesis project I was given access to a lab that has the facilities required to complete my project. The currently enforced access times for this Lab are 8am - 6pm, Monday to Friday. As you are probably aware, I work three days a week for Transport and Main Roads in Nerang, thus limiting my lab time. I brought this issue up very early in the semester and I still have yet to get the access times extended to the weekends.

Below is a excerpt from an email I send to the lab supervisor this afternoon.

Because the Bachelor of Engineering require 60 days of professional practice, I went searching for employment that would satisfy that need. I now work for Queensland Main Roads. This job is three days per week, 8 till 5. This works in well as my scheduled classes lie on the remaining 2 days. However, this does not leave me very much time at all for access to 47-401 to work on the practical aspects of my ENGG4801 Thesis project; perhaps 8 hours a week assuming I don’t take any breaks between thesis work and classes.

As you could probably appreciate, this is not an ideal situation for me. Therefore, I am requesting that access to the Thesis Lab be extended such that I am able to make better use of its facilities. For example, extending the access to include weekends would be very beneficial for students in situations like mine. I don’t necessarily require 24/7 access, but reasonable access on the weekend would be greatly appreciated.

Having met the supervisor in person, he said he will use this case to hopefully appeal to the powers that be, and get the lab access extended.

I should point out two very annoying aspects of this situation:

 

  1. It is half-way through the semester and this situation has still to be taken care of.
  2. There is a course of second and third year students that have 24 hour access to a lab in the same building!

 

Unfortunately, there is not a lot I can do but wait and see the outcome the appeal - apparently set for Tuesday next week. I will give an update to this issue, hopefully one that involves success, when developments occur.
