I have a feeling a couple of people in Australia have no got iPads. So I thought I’d suggest a few Apps that everyone might find useful.

GMail

This is not an app available from the iTunes store, but a web app I save added to my Home Screen. On my iPhone, I use the Apple Mail App. But on the iPad, Google has made a fantastic web app that, in my opinion, is much better to use than the Mail App.

free and paid version (in-app Upgrade). The paid version removes ads and allows multiple accounts. If you do want picture posting and some more advanced features, TweetDeck would be my next best recommendation.

Evernote

I’ve shown off the Evernote app before. It is very good interface to the Evernote service, and has the advantage of storing notes locally and syncing them either on demand or whenever you have internet connectivity.

Free.

Dropbox

I use the Dropbox service quite heavily and the iPad app really opens up great new uses for it. It doesn’t store everything on your iPad by default (though perhaps that would be a good option for an update?) but it does let you Star items you want to keep for offline use. It’s integrated view can handle heaps of file type, including images, MS Office Documents, PDFs, MP3s, Videos etc. The app also gives you the option to export files to other applications that you have that are capable of reading different filetypes. E.g. You can export PDFs to the GoodReader app if you want a more feature rich PDF viewing experience.

Free

GoodReader for iPad

I use GoodReader for reading PDFs and other documents that I need to spend time on. This is because Dropbox doesn’t really let you dispense with the rest of its interface when reading. It is very full featured and I certainly haven’t discovered it’s full potential yet. It does have few IU bugs that force me to exit and reopen the app from time-to-time.

App Store

Wolfram Alpha

This app is amazing. It gives you full access to the Wolfram Alpha engine with a better interface than the web interface. I won’t delve into its features here, but check out the screenshots to see what I mean!

 

App Store.

Mobile Mouse

This is a great app for remotely controlling your Mac or PC from your iPad. It gives you an iPad-sized touchpad and/or a keyboard. It also gives you quick access to your Dock or Taskbar for app-switching. MobileMouse also provides controls for web browsers and media applications. I use these to easily control iTunes.

App Store

TWiTPad

I’m a big fan of the TWiT.tv network, and they have a really good iPad app. It shows live video, live chat and the live schedule.

 

App Store.

Plants vs Zombies HD

I can’t resist putting at least one game up here. This game is so much fun, and you can find yourself waisting alot of time playing it!

App Store. I just realised I didn’t pay that much, because I bought it when the iPad first came out from the US App Store… but its still really fun!
