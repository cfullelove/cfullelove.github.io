Over the last week I have been listening to Parallel Worlds, a book written by Michio Kaku. In this book, Michio Kaku explores the origin, development and future on the universe. It is similar in content, i suppose, to A Brief History of Time by Stephen Hawking, that I read earlier this month, though perhaps a little more current. Yes, apparently I am overly interested in physics…

I very much enjoyed this book. Michio Kaku is very gifted in his ability to translate complex thoughts and ideas into a form that a mildly intelligent person can easily understand. My favourite example of this ability is how Kaku often relates the idea in a Science Fiction context. Perhaps this is because I hold more than just a small soft-spot for SciFi - but for me at least it made the content of this book far easier to understand.

As for the topics covered in this book, they range from the historical perception of the universe, to the current understanding of how the universe looks and behaves.

String theory is discussed quite extensively. Michio discusses the origins of String Theory, the various versions that sprung into existence, and the emergence of M-Theory, which served to largely unify these versions into a single form. This lead onto the discussion on the idea of the existence of higher dimensions and the various theories and ideas that revolve around them.

Interesting philosophical questions raised by various theories and discoveries are discussed too. On more than a couple of occasions the existence and nature of god is covered, especially in relation to the beginning or creation of the universe.

I definitely recommend this book to anyone interested in physics, especially cosmology, particle physics with a hint of philosophy. Please let me know your thought on this book if you have read it. If you have read a book covering a similar topic, please share!
