In the last few days Facebook has been rolling out a new profile page design. It’s supposed to give the Facebook user a better overview of a person’s profile as well as adding a few new features.

One thing I love is that those stupid “tabs” or “boxes” seem to have disappeared. So a Facebook profile page now looks much cleaner and more elegant.

You can read more in-depth reports about the ins and outs on the Facebook Blog and this article in the Sydney Morning Herald. 

Interestingly, this new design is opt in. Meaning that you can choose to use it or just stay the same. There is a considerable amount of commentary about how this is quite out of character for Facebook…who usually force changes on its users. Is Facebook learning? Is it mistake not to force improvements on users?

If you do want to give it a shot, go to this url: http://www.facebook.com/about/profile (Keep in mind that its a one way thing - no going back - like taking the “red pill”) 

To give you a quick visual on the changes, here is a screen cap of my profile (view when I’m logged in) and my brother’s profile - to show you how others’ profiles will look like.

Will be interesting to see if this new design catches on. Please feel free to share your thoughts!
