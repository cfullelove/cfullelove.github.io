It is amazing how much people these days spend infront of a computer, plugged into an iPod, or browsing the web from some other device. I, perhaps sadly, am definitely no exception to this phenomenon. In actual fact I'm more likely worse than average. There are quite a number of things that keep me busy when I'm "plugged in", and I'd like to share a few of those here.
Email
I am an email junkie. I check my inbox every chance I get. I would describe receiving an email as a similar feeling that a child gets when they get a letter in the mail (snail mail that is, before they turn into bills). It is strange. I am definitely not a child, and the novelty has yet to wear off. The only time I don't get excited about an email is if I see it comes from my boss... and I think that is perfectly understandable!
I use Google's GMail as my primary email client. All my other email accounts link through here because, in my opinion, GMail is the best email client for me. I do have a Hotmail account and an account with both my ISP and UQ.
Facebook
I do use Facebook quite alot as well. I use it to keep in contact with my friends mostly. It is also the mean most of my friends use to organise gatherings, parties, holidays etc. I would hate to think how many people I would loose contact with if Facebook didn't exist.
My Facebook url: http://www.facebook.com/cfullelove
Podcasts
Listening to and watching podcasts is one of my favourite things to do in my spare time. I have two different types of podcast I listen to. Firstly, I have the podcasts that deliver news and discussion on recent events that interest me. Some of these are below:


  • TWiT (This Week in Tech):This show, hosted my Leo Laporte, features prominent figures in the tech community discussing the last week's tech news. It usually runs for about 90 mins.
  • TWiG (This Week in Google): TWiG is also from the TWiT network. It is hosted by Leo Laporte, Gina Trapani and Jeff Jarvis. As the name suggests, the discussion in this show revolves around the weekly news as it relates to Google.
  • You Can't Make This Stuff Up: This is a CNN podcast by Jim Ribble. Weekly, Jim Ribble releases a 3-4 minute report on the news that is so unbelievable it must be true. Shows the lighter side of the news.
  • Diggnation: I've only recently subscribed to this podcast, but I do find it very interesting. Its hosted by Keven Rose and Alex Albrecht, and its an hour discussing the top news stories featured on digg.
There are a few other podcasts I subscribe to which are less news orientated. They focus more on education and interest and tend to still be relevant if I don't get around to listening/watching them straight away:
  • Dr Karl's Greatest Moments in Science: Hosted by Dr Karl (@DoctorKarl), this podcast feature 5-10min long explorations into odd topics in science. Explained in layman's terms, good for those not familiar with the topics covered.
  • 60 Second Science: Scientific American releases these 60 second snippets of science every couple of days. They obviously don't go into much depth, but they can be quite interesting and informative.
  • TEDTalks: These talks are almost always interesting. Great minds speaking for up to 20 minutes about a topic that they are passionate about. They aren't always about tech or science, but quite often are thought provoking just the same.

Google Reader
I use Google Reader extensively. Google Reader aggregates all the feeds that I have subscribed to and lets me view them from a browser or on a mobile. I subscribe to many different kinds of feeds: Blogs, News, Web Comics, and more. I have too many feeds to easily show them here, but you can follow items I share here.
