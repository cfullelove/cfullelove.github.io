LinkedIn is basically social networking for the professional. It allows for users to connect with each other on a professional level, leaving the drunken party photos and bogan friend comments to other services like Facebook.

 

I have always stayed away from LinkedIn because I didn’t really have a use for it - mainly because I have never been a professional. Recently, however, I realised that I kinda do qualify as a professional - I work at a real corporate entity as a professional officer (engineer). So I signed up.

First impressions were not far from what I expected. The interface definitely isn’t built to please the greater public. It basic, bare, and not very cuddly like a Facebook or Twitter. But then it isn’t supposed to be.

It does do some things quite well. It handles the input of detailed employment history, contact information, Education details, etc quite well. It almost allows you to build a stripped down resume, but then - because its a social network - allows you to share your profile with colleges, friends and associates.

Apparently, the use of LinkedIn isn’t as limited as some might believe. I noticed that quite a number of people I know professionally or otherwise use the service. In fact some use the service because its expected - not just because they want to.

I’m quite interested to see how I use LinedIn in the near future. Perhaps I might be able to do some serious networking, perhaps even help me in the search for further employment… In any case I will definitely post again should anything interesting come of its use.
