Google Juice is a measurement of how popular a website is in the eyes of Google. Google ranks a page by, among other metrics, measuring the number and quality of the links to that page. The better the content on the page, it follows that there will be more links.


The concept of Google Juice is covered in detail in Jeff Javis’ book, What Would Google Do?, if you are looking for a good read on the topic.

The reason I mention this is because I recently made a interesting discovery. I googled distracted engineer and this blog was the first result!
I’m not sure this result will hold for everyone, but still, its a start. Of course it could mean that there rest of the results are insanely googlejuiceless and that Distracted Engineer is just the best of a bad batch. But I think I’ll feign blissful ignorance of that for the time being.
