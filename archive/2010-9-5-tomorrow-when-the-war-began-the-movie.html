--- layout: post title: "Tomorrow When the War Began - The Movie" post_date: 2010-09-05 13:14:31 ---

 

I just got back from seeing the new Aussie film “Tomorrow When the War Began” based on the John Marsden book of the same name. I am a very big fan of the Tomorrow Series having read - or more accurately listened to - all seven of the books. To be honest, I went into the cinema worried they might not do a very good job. But now, I think the job they have done is fantastic.

The movie is based on the first book in the Tomorrow Series, and follows a bunch of Australian teens through a pretty amazing story. Here is the IMDB synopsis:

Based on the first of seven books by acclaimed Australian author, John Marsden, Tomorrow When The War Began is the story of seven teenagers who return from a week-long camping trip to find that Australia has been invaded by a foreign power.

Banding together to fight gurellia-style agaist the enemy, this is not a typical heroic war movie - it is a terrifying situation where they must sometimes use little more than a knife or a belt to murder a soldier in cold blood. It’s a graphic tale of the violence, the blood, the fear, and the insanity of war.

These kids are underdogs, they’re not going to win the war, they’re more likely to end up dead in a ditch than send the enemy retreating, but it’s their land, their parents and friends in prison camps. They give a damn and they’d rather die fighting than give up.

Something that made me nervous about this film was that it is an Australian production with Australian actors. And while I am all for Australian film in principal, I didn’t want to be.. well.. crap. Thankfully this definitely wasn’t the case in my opinion - even with cast members from Neighbours and Home and Away.

The movie took me completely by surprise - shocked me with its intensity - but I won’t say too much more lest I spoil it. I really liked how I recognised heaps of quotes from dialogue appearing in the text. The cast too, I thought, did quite well and fitted well with how I pictured them to be when reading the books.

There were a couple of things I wasn’t wild about. I wish they could have delved deeper into the emotions of the group members a bit more. In the book a lot of time is spent describing the thoughts and feelings of the characters in between all the action - a big part of the story deals with the utter horror of war. Also, I’m not sure how easy the movie would have been to follow if I had not read the book previously. I would love comments from those who haven’t read the book to see what they thought.

If you have read any of the Tomorrow Series books I strongly suggest you go see this movie - you will love it. If you haven’t I still think you will enjoy it a lot - perhaps even persuade you to read the books. Very keen to hear what others thought about this film. Please leave your comments below!
