Yet another book I recently finished that I would like to review a little is “Surely You’re Joking Mr Feynman!” by Richard P. Feynman. This book was nothing like I expected. As Feynman was a very well known and successful physicist, I expected this book to be far more physics based. Instead, it consisted of a collection of incredibly interesting personal stories throughout Feynman’s life. Obviously some of these stories did relate to physics, but the physical concepts weren’t the point of these stories.

On reading this book, I discovered there is a lot more to Richard Feynman than I had originally realised. Feynman discusses his involvement in the atomic bomb’s development, the various universities he attended and taught at, his exploits in Brazil, adventures with girls, music, art and philosophy.

Feynman came across as an incredibly interesting and, at times, hilarious character. A number of his stories proved to be very thought provoking too. This was largely due to Feynman’s very unique point of view in life. Even stories that would otherwise had been quite dull, were made interesting, funny even, by viewing it from his interesting angle.

Once again, I listened to this book as an audiobook from Audible.com. While I did find the stories in the book very interesting by themselves, I did find that they were a little disjointed. I couldn’t follow the stories in a distinct chronological order. This didn’t detract to much from the experience, but it was definitely noticable for me, and for a few reviewers that posted on Audible.

I do recommend this book to anyone interested in science in general as well as just a good read and a good laugh. I thank Eloise King-Smith for recommending I read this book, was definitely worth it.
